Planetary Gearbox Optimization

For a high-torque robotic actuator, I needed a compact reduction system that could fit inside a 50mm diameter tube. A planetary gearset was the obvious choice due to its high power density. The design required a 4:1 reduction ratio. The primary challenge was selecting the correct module and number of teeth to ensure the sun, planets, and ring gear assembled correctly without interference.

I performed a theoretical stress analysis to verify that 3D printed Nylon gears could withstand the stall torque of the motor. The calculation involved determining the bending stress at the root of the gear tooth using the Lewis formula. I wrote a Python script to iterate through various gear modules and face widths to find the optimal balance between size and strength.

Python - Gear Stress Calculation
def calculate_lewis_stress(force, module, face_width, lewis_form_factor):
    """
    Calculates bending stress based on Lewis Formula
    Sigma = (Wt) / (F * m * Y)
    """
    tangential_load = force # Newtons
    
    # Stress in MPa
    stress = tangential_load / (face_width * module * lewis_form_factor)
    
    return stress

# Example: 50N load, Module 1.0, 10mm width, Y=0.3
current_stress = calculate_lewis_stress(50, 1.0, 10, 0.3)
print(f"Bending Stress: {current_stress:.2f} MPa")

After the math was verified, I moved to Finite Element Analysis (FEA) in SolidWorks Simulation to validate the results. The FEA heatmap showed stress concentrations at the root fillets consistent with my calculations. The final design was printed in NylonX (carbon-fiber reinforced nylon), assembled, and successfully tested at 5Nm of torque without failure.
